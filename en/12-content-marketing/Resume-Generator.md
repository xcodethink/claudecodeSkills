# Tailored Resume Generator

## Overview

This skill analyzes job descriptions to extract key requirements, then generates customized resumes that highlight the most relevant experience, skills, and achievements. It optimizes for both human readers and ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems), provides gap analysis, and offers strategic recommendations for cover letters and interview preparation.

## When to Use

- Applying for a specific job position
- Customizing your resume for different industries or roles
- Highlighting relevant experience for career transitions
- Optimizing your resume for ATS systems
- Creating multiple resume versions for different applications
- Emphasizing specific skills mentioned in job postings

## What It Does

1. **Analyzes Job Descriptions** -- Extracts key requirements, skills, qualifications, and keywords
2. **Identifies Priorities** -- Determines what employers value most based on language and structure
3. **Tailors Content** -- Reorganizes and emphasizes relevant experience and achievements
4. **Optimizes Keywords** -- Incorporates ATS-friendly keywords naturally
5. **Formats Professionally** -- Creates clean, scannable layouts
6. **Provides Recommendations** -- Suggests improvements and highlights gaps

## How It Works

### 1. Gather Information

**Job Description Analysis**:
- Request the full job description if not provided
- Ask for the company name and job title

**Candidate Background**:
- If existing resume provided, use as foundation
- Otherwise request: work history, education, skills, achievements, certifications

### 2. Analyze Job Requirements

Extract and prioritize:
- **Priority 1**: Critical requirements (deal-breakers) -- years of experience, required skills, education
- **Priority 2**: Important qualifications (strongly desired) -- key technical tools, methodologies
- **Priority 3**: Nice-to-have skills (bonus points) -- soft skills, industry knowledge, certifications

Also extract: repeated keywords, company values, and cultural fit indicators.

### 3. Map Experience to Requirements

For each job requirement:
- Identify matching experience from candidate background
- Find transferable skills if no direct match
- Note gaps to address or de-emphasize
- Identify unique strengths to highlight

### 4. Structure the Tailored Resume

**Professional Summary** (3-4 lines):
- Lead with years of experience in the target field
- Include top 3-4 required skills from job description
- Mention industry experience if relevant
- Highlight unique value proposition

**Technical/Core Skills Section**:
- Group skills by category matching job requirements
- List required tools and technologies first
- Use exact terminology from job description

**Professional Experience**:
- Emphasize responsibilities and achievements aligned with job requirements
- Use action verbs: Led, Developed, Implemented, Optimized, Managed, Created, Analyzed
- Quantify achievements with numbers, percentages, timeframes, scale
- Reorder bullet points to prioritize most relevant experience
- Format: **[Action Verb] + [What] + [How/Why] + [Result/Impact]**

**Education**: Degrees, certifications, relevant coursework.

**Optional Sections**: Certifications, Publications, Awards, Volunteer Work, Projects.

### 5. ATS Optimization

- Use standard section headings (Professional Experience, Education, Skills)
- Incorporate exact keywords from job description naturally
- Avoid tables, graphics, headers/footers, or complex formatting
- Use standard fonts and bullet points
- Include both acronyms and full terms (e.g., "SQL (Structured Query Language)")

### 6. Format Guidelines

- 1 page for less than 10 years experience, 2 pages for 10+ years
- Consistent formatting and spacing
- Prominent contact information
- Reverse chronological order
- Clean, scannable layout with white space

## Strategic Recommendations

After presenting the tailored resume, the skill provides:

**Strengths Analysis**: What makes the candidate competitive, unique qualifications to emphasize in interviews.

**Gap Analysis**: Requirements not fully met, suggestions for addressing gaps (courses, projects, reframing experience).

**Interview Preparation Tips**: Key talking points aligned with the resume, stories to prepare, questions to ask.

**Cover Letter Hooks**: 2-3 suggested opening lines, key achievements to expand upon.

## Special Considerations

### Career Changers
- Use functional or hybrid resume format
- Emphasize transferable skills
- Create compelling narrative in summary
- Focus on relevant projects and coursework

### Recent Graduates
- Lead with education
- Include relevant coursework, projects, internships
- Emphasize leadership in student organizations
- Include GPA if 3.5+

### Senior Executives
- Lead with executive summary
- Focus on leadership and strategic impact
- Include board memberships, speaking engagements
- Emphasize revenue growth, team building, vision

### Technical Roles
- Include technical skills section prominently
- List programming languages, frameworks, tools
- Include GitHub, portfolio, or project links
- Mention methodologies (Agile, Scrum, etc.)

## Best Practices

**Do**:
- Be truthful and accurate
- Use industry-standard terminology
- Quantify achievements with specific metrics
- Tailor each resume to the specific job
- Proofread for grammar and consistency

**Don't**:
- Include personal information (age, marital status, photo)
- Use first-person pronouns (I, me, my)
- Include "references available upon request"
- List every job if career is 20+ years
- Use generic templates without customization
- Exceed 2 pages unless very senior role

## Tips for Best Results

- **Be specific** -- Provide complete job descriptions and detailed background
- **Share metrics** -- Include numbers, percentages, and quantifiable achievements
- **Indicate format preference** -- ATS-optimized, creative, or traditional
- **Mention constraints** -- Page limits, sections to include/exclude
- **Iterate** -- Ask for revisions or alternative approaches
- **Multiple applications** -- Generate separate tailored versions for different roles
